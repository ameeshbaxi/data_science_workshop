What is the True Normal Human Body Temperature?¶

Background¶

The mean normal body temperature was held to be 37$^{\circ}$C or 98.6$^{\circ}$F for more than 120 years since it was first conceptualized and reported by Carl Wunderlich in a famous 1868 book. But, is this value statistically correct?

Answer-3¶

Is the true population mean really 98.6 degrees F?

  1. Population mean is given. To see if the given sample is inline with population mean, one-sample test will be used.
  2. Since poluation std deviation or variance is not given, t-test will be used for this purpose.

Take a null hypothesis to determine that the given sample is matching to the population mean 98.6 Degrees F. The null hypothesis (H0) assumes that the difference between the true mean (μ) and the comparison value (m0) is equal to zero.

In [6]:
m = 98.6
t_static, p_value = stats.ttest_1samp(df.temperature, m)
print ('t-statistic = %6.3f pvalue = %6.4f' %  (t_static, p_value))

t-statistic = -5.455 pvalue = 0.0000
